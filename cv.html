Hands on Experience

To execute a project successfully, a solid plan must be in place. This often means creating a mockup of the software and having it signed off by the client before wiring it up.

I take a slightly different approach. Before I get to the mockup stage, I need to truly understand my clients business and their need for software. I'll give you some real examples:

Asbestos Management a huge project

I was asked to create a system to collate and deliver asbestos surveys.

  • I spent 2 weeks studying each department in the company. Some departments weren't formally defined, so we did that as we went:
    • higher management
    • accounts management
    • sales
    • laboratory
    • admin
    • field-work
    • fleet management
    • finances
  • After compiling a process diagram representing the flow of information through company, I then creaed systems to solve key issues.

Personal Training App a mid-sized project

I was approached by a group of Personal Trainers to create a mobile app. They wanted to deliver curated workouts to their clients.

I asked them to take me on as a client and started training. Over a few training sessions, and a few "normal" meetings we concluded that delivering workouts would erode the experience of a face-to-face training. The app we created was aligned with their business goals and promoted continued training with the group.

Technical Abilities

Picking the best tool for the job is a critical part of any project. I like to review the available languages and frameworks on a case-by-case basis.

For larger projects, it can be easy to swallow the "cost" of learning new technologies by reviewing the value on offer. A recent example comes to mind, I had to decide on the right database to use for a mobile app that synchronised data to a server:

While I am familiar with MongoDB, MySQL and RDS - CouchDB + PouchDB offers mobile data synchronisation out of the box.

The cost of learning CouchDB + PouchDB will be lower than dealing with data synchronisation on the databases I already know.
