Stone Soup is a folk tale that our Grandad used to tell us as children, it's about a little old biddy who persuades a community to come together and make "stone soup" on the grounds that it's "only a stone and hot water"... of course once the salt, pepper, potatoes, onions, mutton, carrots, split-peas, stock, turnips, celery, cream and barley are added the stone soup is truly successful.

It's also a lie.

I have carried around the anology for years, I use it to describe how complex software evolves from it's simple stone and water conception to it's final broth rich with flavours.

There are clients that are too naive or ignorant to understand what they want and believe in their own "simple" demands, and then there are clients that know exactly the complex broth they want and are using the "simple" starting point to trick you into making it.
